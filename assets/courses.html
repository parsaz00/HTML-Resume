List of Relevant Courses Taken at UBC in Both my Degrees

Bachelor Of Computer Science

  1. CPSC 103 Introduction to Systematic Program Design

    2. Computation as a tool for systematic problem solving in non-computer-science disciplines. Introductory programming skills.

  2. CPSC 110 Computation, Programs, and Programming

    3. Fundamental program and computation structures. Introductory programming skills. Computation as a tool for information processing, simulation and modelling, and interacting with the world.

  3. CPSC 121 Models of Computation

    4. Physical and mathematical structures of computation. Boolean algebra and combinations logic circuits; proof techniques; functions and sequential circuits; sets and relations; finite state machines; sequential instruction execution.

  4. CPSC 210 Software Construction

    5. Design, development, and analysis of robust software components. Topics such as software design, computational models, data structures, debugging, and testing. Object Oriented Programing.

  5. CPSC 213 Introduction to Computer Systems

    6. Software architecture, operating systems, and I/O architectures. Relationships between application software, operating systems, and computing hardware; critical sections, deadlock avoidance, and performance; principles and operation of disks and networks.

  6. CPSC 221 Basic Algorithms and Data Structures

    7. Design and analysis of basic algorithms and data structures; algorithm analysis methods, searching and sorting algorithms, basic data structures, graphs and concurrency.

Bachelor of Science, Behavioural Neuroscience

  1. PSYC 472 Advanced Neuroscience of Motivation

    2. Neurobiological pathways mediating five core aspects of motivated behaviour: (1) affective decision making; (2) motivated drive; (3) goal-directed vs. habit-based behaviour; (4) learning from negative reinforcers; and (5) disorders of motivation.

  2. PSYC 462 Drugs and Behavioural Neuroscience

    3. Introduction to neurochemical systems and functional neuroanatomy; animal models of human cognitive processes and mental disorders; neurochemical foundations and treatments for mental disorders.

  3. PSYC 460 Behavioural Neuroendocrinology

    4. Detailed examination of the interaction between hormones and neural control of reproductive and non-reproductive behaviours; emphasis on gonadal and adrenal hormone effects on learning and memory in the brain.

  4. PSYC 371 Behavioural and Cognitive Neuroscience II

    5. Neurodevelopment and neuroplasticity, the physiological bases of several motivated behaviours (sleep, sexual behaviour, hunger and eating, drugs and addiction), cognition, the stress response, and brain dysfunction and recovery.

  5. PSYC 370 Behavioural and Cognitive Neuroscience I

    6. Advanced methods and topics in behavioural and cognitive neuroscience.

  6. PSYC 368 Perceptual Processing

    7. Perceptual phenomena and their underlying brain mechanisms.

  7. PSYC 367 Sensory Systems

    8. Anatomy and physiology of the sensory pathways and their relation to perception.

  8. PSYC 365 Cognitive Neuroscience

    9. Current research into human brain systems underlying cognitive processes such as perception, attention, memory, and decision-making.

  9. PSYC 363 Neuroscience of Simple Learning

    10. Introduction to basic theories of non-associative learning, classical and operant conditioning. Experimental findings from behavioural and biological analyses with animals and humans.
